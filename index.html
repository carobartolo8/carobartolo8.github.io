Hello,

My name is Carolina Bartolo, I am currently a political science major interested in studying immigration law. My main inspiration to study immigration lawyer is to help and educate marginalized hispanic communities. Another inspiration of mine were my parents because they went through a tedious process in order to obtain naturalization. Immigration lawyers advise clients on legal laws and procedures that may affect their legal journey for naturalization, work-visas, and family based immigration. Immigration lawyers also represent clients in immigration courts and administrative hearings, and before U.S citizenship and immigration services. The day as immigration lawyer may include drafting legal documents like motions and briefs, preparing clients for interviews with immigration or court hearings, and conducting research to stay up to date with immigration laws and regulations.

My current professional experience includes working for a non-profit organization called Inland Legal Services located in riverside, california. I had an internship with this non profit organization and I worked closely with immigration lawyers and clients. My main priority was listening to a client and writing down their statement on why they were seeking legal advice. I had to accurately and efficiently communicate this with the immigration lawyers. In order to do this I would fill client intake reports that included their statement, contact information, and demographics. My other job was to draft briefs and prepare a client to speak with the lawyer so both parties were on the same page. The main community that seeks legal advice was the hispanic community so I had to use my bilingual skills while working at the office.

When in court the clients were often nervous because they feared if they were denied naturalization they would be deported. We would prepare them as much as we could in order for them to feel comfortable with the court hearings decision. I would have long days but it was all worth it because I felt like I made a great impact in many families. My family went through the same process when I was younger and it was really difficult navigating the journey as a first generation.

I believe that going to law school will give me the opportunity to help many families and communities achieve their american dream. Many families just like mine come to the United States for the opportunities that they cannot receive back home. Many families pay taxes, work, and stay away from trouble in hopes of one day being an American citizen. My goal is to one day be able to make the dreams of many hardworking communities become true.

Ultimately, I believe that a job as an immigration lawyer will make an impact for many families and make my dream of helping others become true.
